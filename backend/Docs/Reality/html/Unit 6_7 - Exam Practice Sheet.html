1. Shock – Types, Key Signs, Priority Actions

Types of Shock

Cardiogenic
  • Typical Causes: Acute MI, severe LV failure, cardiomyopathy, dysrhythmias, structural damage.
  • Key Signs: Tachycardia, hypotension, tachypnea, cool/clammy skin, signs of peripheral hypoperfusion, crackles (if LV failure).
  • Priority Actions: High-flow O₂, continuous monitoring, IV access, prepare for inotropes/vasopressors, rapid cardiology input.
Hypovolemic (Absolute/Relative)
  • Typical Causes: Hemorrhage, severe trauma, burns, pancreatitis, massive GI loss, third spacing.
  • Key Signs: Tachycardia, hypotension, ↑ RR & depth, ↓ urine output, cool/pale skin, anxiety.
  • Priority Actions: Control source of loss, aggressive IV fluid resuscitation, monitor vitals/urine output closely.
Neurogenic (Distributive)
  • Typical Causes: Spinal cord injury at/above T5, spinal anesthesia, severe CNS depressants.
  • Key Signs: Hypotension with bradycardia, warm/dry skin, inability to regulate temperature.
  • Priority Actions: Maintain spinal precautions, airway support, cautious fluids, vasopressors, prevent hypothermia.
Anaphylactic (Distributive)
  • Typical Causes: Drugs, blood products, insect stings, foods, latex.
  • Key Signs: Sudden airway swelling, wheeze/stridor, dyspnea, flushing/urticaria, "impending doom".
  • Priority Actions: IM epinephrine immediately, high-flow O₂, call rapid response, prepare fluids/steroids/antihistamines.
Septic (Distributive)
  • Typical Causes: Advanced/untreated infection, high-risk clients.
  • Key Signs: Hypotension, tachycardia, altered LOC, fever/hypothermia, elevated lactate.
  • Priority Actions: Early sepsis protocol, obtain cultures, broad-spectrum antibiotics ASAP, aggressive IV fluids.

General Shock Assessment Checklist

  • Mental status: Anxiety → restlessness → confusion → ↓ LOC → unresponsive.
  • BP & Pulse: ≥20 mm Hg drop from baseline; tachycardic, thready pulse.
  • Respirations: Rapid, shallow; SpO₂ often <90% in late stages.
  • Temperature: Febrile (septic); low/normal in others.
  • Skin: Cool, pale, mottled; delayed cap refill.
  • Urine output: Decreased (aim ≥0.5 mL/kg/hr).

2. CAD & Chest Pain – Fast Reference

CAD & Risk Factors

  • Definition: CAD = atherosclerotic narrowing of coronary arteries → ischemia/infarction.
  • Non-modifiable: Age ↑, male <65, Black ethnicity, family history.
  • Major modifiable: Dyslipidemia (↑ LDL, ↓ HDL), HTN ≥140/90, smoking, inactivity, central obesity.

Angina vs ACS vs MI

Chronic Stable Angina (CSA)
  • Pattern: Predictable, exertional, pressure/ache, lasts 3–5 min. Relieved by rest or nitro.
  • Context: Fixed atherosclerotic narrowing.
  • Actions: Stop activity, assess PQRST, give nitro/O₂.
Unstable Angina (UA) / ACS
  • Pattern: New, at rest, or worsening. Unpredictable, less responsive to nitro.
  • Context: Plaque rupture with non-occlusive thrombus.
  • Actions: Treat as emergency; 12-lead ECG, O₂, nitro, ASA, IV access.
STEMI / NSTEMI (MI)
  • Pattern: Severe, crushing, >20 min. Not relieved by rest/nitro. Diaphoresis, N/V.
  • Context: Sustained ischemia with myocardial necrosis.
  • Actions: MONA + ECG (Morphine, O₂, Nitro, ASA), prep for reperfusion (PCI).
Chest Pain Assessment: PQRST - Precipitating factors, Quality, Radiation, Severity (0–10), Timing.

3. Cardiac Rhythms – Key Patterns & Priorities

ECG Basics

  • P wave: Atrial depolarization.
  • PR interval: Impulse through atria → AV node → His–Purkinje.
  • QRS: Ventricular depolarization.
  • ST segment: Early repolarization (should be isoelectric).

Rhythm Quick Reference

Sinus Bradycardia
  • Hallmark: Regular, <60 bpm.
  • Actions: Assess symptoms. If unstable: O₂, atropine/pacing.
Atrial Fibrillation (AF)
  • Hallmark: Irregularly irregular, no P waves.
  • Actions: Assess stability. Rate control & anticoagulation.
Ventricular Tachycardia (VT)
  • Hallmark: Wide, fast QRS; ≥3 PVCs.
  • Actions: No pulse = CPR + Defibrillation. Pulse (unstable) = Synchronized cardioversion.
Ventricular Fibrillation (VF)
  • Hallmark: Chaotic, irregular waveform; quivering.
  • Actions: Immediate CPR + Defibrillation (ACLS protocols).
Asystole
  • Hallmark: Flat line.
  • Actions: CPR, Epinephrine. NO defibrillation.

Dysrhythmia Emergency Checklist (Unstable Rhythm)

  • 1. CAB: Check responsiveness/pulse. Start CPR if pulseless.
  • 2. Airway & Breathing: Ensure airway, give O₂, prep for intubation.
  • 3. Circulation: Attach monitor/defibrillator, establish IV.
  • 4. ACLS: Follow algorithm for specific rhythm.
