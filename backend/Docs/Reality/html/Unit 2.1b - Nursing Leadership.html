Comprehensive Study Sheet

1. REx-PN Exam Preparation & Strategy

  • Study Plan: Create a realistic schedule balancing clinicals, studying, personal commitments, and intentional rest periods.
  • Test Anxiety Management: Utilize deep slow breathing to oxygenate the brain; build confidence through repeated exposure to practice questions (Adaptive Mock Exams/CAT).
Test-Taking Strategies:
  • Treat each question as a separate problem; do not let difficult questions cause anxiety.
  • Do not make assumptions unless directly implied by the question.
  • Read carefully, spend no more than 1–2 minutes per question. Your first choice is often correct.

2. Key Concepts in Nursing Leadership

  • Transformational Leadership: Emphasizes that a leader with vision and values can inspire others. Strongly contributes to healthy work environments and nurse retention.
  • Trauma-Informed Leadership: Aims to create safety, build trust through transparency, empower voice/choice, and critically examine systems perpetuating harm.
  • Collaborative Practice: Multiple health workers from different professional backgrounds working together with patients/families/communities to deliver high-quality care.
  • Continuity of Care: Seamless, continuous implementation of a care plan, reviewed and revised to meet changing client needs.
  • Case Management: Collaborative process assessing, planning, implementing, coordinating, and evaluating services to meet health needs cost-effectively.

3. Delegation vs. Assignment

  • Delegation: Active process of transferring responsibility for an intervention to someone whose scope of practice does not normally authorize it.
  • Assignment: Allocating care and interventions that are within that individual's authorized scope.
  • Crucial Rule of Accountability: The nurse who delegates retains accountability for monitoring/evaluating outcomes. The delegate is responsible for performing the task safely.
  • Important Restrictions: Nurses do not delegate to other nurses (e.g., RNs do not delegate to LPNs; they assign).
Criteria for Safe Delegation:
  • Task must have a readily predictable outcome.
  • Poses low risk to the client.
  • Uses a standard process.
  • Requires no ongoing assessment or independent decision-making during the task.

4. Prioritization Frameworks (Essential for Clinical Judgment)

Acuity vs. Intensity:
  • Acuity: Level of care required based on severity of illness/condition (e.g., 1 = stable; 4 = high risk/frequent interventions).
  • Intensity: Amount of time needed to complete care (e.g., ADLs, wound care).
Key Frameworks:
  • Maslow’s Hierarchy: Prioritize foundational physiological needs (oxygen, fluid, excretion) before safety, emotional/belonging, esteem, and self-actualization.
  • ABCs (Airway, Breathing, Circulation): Most critical physiological needs for preserving human life. Always assess and intervene here first.
  • CURE Hierarchy:
    C - Critical: Requires immediate intervention to prevent deterioration or death.
